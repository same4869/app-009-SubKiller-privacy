SubKiller — Privacy Policy

订阅斩 隐私政策（English Version）

Local-First No Ad Tracking No Third-Party SDKs Effective: January 1, 2025
Our Core Promise: SubKiller is a local-first app. Your subscription data, spending records, and usage habits are stored only on your device (or synced to your private iCloud account with your explicit permission). We do not collect, upload, or sell any personal data.

📋Data We Handle

Data Type Storage Purpose
Subscription records (name, amount, date) Device / iCloudlocal Core app functionality and reminders
App settings (currency, salary, reminder prefs) Device / iCloudlocal Personalized calculations and notifications
Screen Time data (app usage duration) Device onlylocal Usage Insight feature (requires your explicit authorization)
Camera / photo images (OCR scanning) Not storedlocal Recognized in memory only; discarded immediately after parsing
Local notifications Device onlylocal Billing reminders (requires your permission)
Crash / diagnostic data Apple platformoptional Collected by Apple if you have opted in to share diagnostics with developers in device settings

🚫What We Never Collect

  • Personal identity information (name, phone, email, etc.)
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, advertising ID, etc.)
  • Behavioral analytics, click-stream, or usage logs
  • Contacts, calendar, or data from other apps
  • Any data via third-party advertising or analytics SDKs

📱Screen Time (DeviceActivity)

The Usage Insight feature uses Apple's DeviceActivity framework to read app usage duration. This requires you to grant Screen Time permission.

  • All Screen Time data is processed locally on your device via App Group shared storage between the app and its system extension
  • We only read app display names and usage minutes — never app content
  • This data is never transmitted to any server
  • You can revoke permission at any time in Settings; the app will stop reading immediately

☁️iCloud Sync (Pro only)

Pro users may enable iCloud Sync. When enabled, your subscription data is synced via Apple CloudKit across devices signed in with the same Apple ID.

  • Data is stored in your personal CloudKit Private Database — we have no access to it
  • Subject to Apple's Privacy Policy
  • You can manage or delete app data in Settings → Apple ID → iCloud at any time

🔍OCR Magic Scanner

OCR uses Apple's on-device Vision framework. Recognition happens entirely on your device.

  • Images are processed in memory only and released immediately after recognition — never persisted
  • No image or recognition result is sent to any server or third party
  • You can revoke Camera / Photos access in Settings at any time

🔔Local Notifications

SubKiller uses Apple's UNUserNotificationCenter to deliver billing reminders. Notification content is generated entirely on-device and does not pass through any push notification server or contain tracking parameters.

You can disable notifications in Settings → Notifications, or adjust them within the app.

🔌Third-Party SDKs & Services

SubKiller does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

All frameworks used are Apple native system frameworks (SwiftData, SwiftUI, Vision, DeviceActivity, FamilyControls, CloudKit, UserNotifications). Their data handling is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

👶Children's Privacy

SubKiller is designed for users aged 17 and older and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

🗑️Data Deletion

You can delete all your data at any time by:

  • In-app: Profile → Danger Zone → Clear All Data
  • Uninstalling the app (removes all local SwiftData on-device storage)
  • If iCloud Sync is enabled: Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → delete SubKiller data

📝Policy Changes

If we make material changes to this policy, we will notify you prominently within the app and update the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
